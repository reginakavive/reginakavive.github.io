About me

Hi there! I am a passionate data professional with over 8 years of experience in data science, Geographic Information Systems (GIS), and remote sensing. Currently, I work as a GIS Data Scientist at Rainforest Alliance (Kenya) and previously served as Senior Data Analyst at the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) in Kenya and Rwanda. I specialize in statistical and spatial analyses, information systems, and big data analytics, applying these skills to generate actionable insights that inform sustainable development initiatives especially in agri-food systems, environmental conservation and climate resilience. My work is guided by a commitment to applying data-driven approaches that create tangible impact, and I am eager to continue contributing to meaningful solutions in these fields.

As for my background, I hold a bachelor's degree in Geospatial Engineering from the University of Nairobi in Kenya and a Master of Science in Data Science from the University of Rwanda’s African Centre of Excellence for Data Science (ACE-DS), thanks to the World Bank’s ACE II project scholarship program. Over the past five years at Rainforest Alliance and IITA R&D, I have applied data science and remote sensing to agricultural research, focusing on crop mapping, climate-related risk modeling for pests and diseases, diet quality, and decision support for crop management. I have also developed efficient ETL workflows and designed intuitive dashboards to turn complex data into actionable insights. When I am not immersed in algorithms and datasets, I enjoy mentoring, seeking new adventures, exploring the world, and tending to my little garden sanctuary.
